The Scholar's Scroll

An Interactive Story

The Discovery

You are Elara, a dedicated linguistics student exploring the dusty archive of the Grand Library. Hidden behind a stack of brittle volumes, you discover a small, leather-bound scroll tied with faded blue ribbon. You feel a faint humming energy emanating from it.

Do you untie the ribbon now, risking the scroll's fragility, or take it to the conservation lab first?

Untie the Ribbon Now Take it to the Lab

A Swift Revelation

You carefully untie the ribbon. The moment the knot loosens, the scroll unfurls itself with a rush of ancient air, revealing a single, glowing symbol. The air hums violently, and you realize you've triggered something. A voice whispers in archaic French: *“Qui cherche la clé?”*

Do you:

Answer the Voice (in French) Run for Help Check the edges for hidden inscriptions

The Official Procedure

You place the scroll in a protective box and take it to the conservation lab. Dr. Dubois examines it with awe and takes custody of the scroll, promising to contact you when they have more information. You wait anxiously for days.

You continue your normal studies, but one morning, a coded email arrives from an unknown sender. The subject is "The Scroll." Do you:

Open the Coded Email Report the Email Secretly try to get access to the scroll and photocopy it.

The Hidden Marginalia

You focus your eyes, noticing faint, minute scratches along the parchment's edge, written in microscopic Latin. They are instructions for safely closing the scroll. As you perform the final step, the glowing symbol winks out, and the humming stops. The scroll is inert but safe.

You have preserved the artifact! Do you take it home to translate the Latin, or report its safe status to the library?

Translate it privately Report its safe status to the library

A Risky Duplication

You sneak into the lab late one night, managing to snap a few high-resolution photos of the scroll before realizing Dr. Dubois has returned to work late. You hide just as Dr. Dubois begins translating the central symbol—it means "Key."

Do you try to sneak out immediately, or wait for Dr. Dubois to leave, hoping to hear more?

Sneak out immediately Wait, and risk being discovered (Leads back to the 'intrigue' path)

The Path of Knowledge (Good Ending)

You confidently answer, *“C'est moi qui la cherche.”* The symbol dims, and a section of the library wall slides open, revealing a hidden, spiral staircase leading down. You have demonstrated intellectual courage and earned access to the library's deepest secrets.
