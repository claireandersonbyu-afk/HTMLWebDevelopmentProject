About Me

I have loved stories as far back as I can remember. I remember sitting on my mom's lap and struggling to figure out the rules of vowel pronounciation when I was certain that I would never be able to figure it out. The first book I remember writing was in first grade and it was an eclectic fanfic of The Nutcracker. Then I realized that I had a talent for it when I was in third grade. I had an amazing teacher who encouraged me and my budding talent (as talented as a third grader can be) and I fell in love with writing. And the rest as they say, was history.

About Writing

Good writing is built upon a foundation of several key elements that work together to create an effective and engaging piece. Clarity is paramount; the language must be precise, the ideas easy to follow, and the structure logical so the reader can grasp the main points without confusion. This clarity is supported by conciseness, which means using the fewest necessary words to convey an idea, eliminating jargon, and avoiding unnecessary repetition. A third vital element is coherence, where all sentences and paragraphs connect smoothly and logically to one another, often achieved through effective use of transition words. Finally, voice and tone are crucial, as they reflect the writer's personality and attitude toward the subject, helping to establish a connection with the audience and make the writing memorable.

Creative writing—whether it's fiction, poetry, or memoir—requires a specific focus on elements that make a narrative come alive, evoke emotion, and immerse the reader in the story's world.

  • Character Development: This is arguably the most crucial element. Characters must feel real, multi-dimensional, and authentic, possessing a complex mix of motivations, flaws, desires, and a believable history (backstory). The story's effectiveness depends on the reader's emotional investment in the characters' journeys and growth (character arc).

  • Show, Don't Tell: Instead of stating facts directly (e.g., "She was angry"), creative writing demands the writer show the emotion through sensory details, actions, and dialogue (e.g., "Her fists clenched, and she slammed the phone onto the desk"). This technique creates a vivid, immediate experience for the reader.

  • Imagery and Sensory Detail: Excellent creative writing appeals to all five senses (sight, sound, smell, taste, and touch). Using vivid, descriptive language, metaphors, and similes paints a rich mental picture and builds a believable and immersive setting.

  • Plot and Conflict: The narrative must have a compelling structure, often following a dramatic arc (exposition, rising action, climax, falling action, resolution). At the heart of this structure is conflict (Character vs. Self, Character vs. Character, Character vs. Nature/Society), which creates tension, drives the plot forward, and forces the characters to change.

  • Voice and Style: This is the unique personality and tone of the writing. A distinctive narrative voice reflects the author's or narrator's attitude toward the material and helps make the writing memorable. The style includes the writer's characteristic use of figurative language, sentence structure, and rhythm.

  • Dialogue: Conversations must sound natural, realistic, and purposeful. Good dialogue not only advances the plot but also reveals character traits, establishes relationships, and adds realism to the scene.

These elements work together to transform a simple story idea into an engaging, emotional, and thought-provoking piece of art.
